# Logistics-Delivery-Delays-Analysis-Dashboard.
Project Overview: 
Efficient logistics operations are critical for ensuring timely product delivery and maintaining customer satisfaction. Delivery delays can negatively impact customer experience, increase operational costs, and reduce trust in logistics network.
This project analyzes delivery performance across multiple categories, and cities to identify patterns in delivery delays.Using data analysis and visualization techniques, the dashboard hihglights key performance metrics, delay trends, and operational inefficiencies. The goal of this project is to provide data-driven insights that can help logistics teams identify delay drivers and improve dilevery performace.
# Business Problem:
Logistics companies must continuously monitor dilevery performance to ensure that orders reach customers on time.However,delays can occur due to operational inefficiencies, warehouse performance differences, or product-specific issues.
This analysis aims to answer key business questions related to dilevery delay and help stakeholders understand where improvements are needed.
# Business Questions:
The project was built to answer the following questions:
(1) Which product categories experience the highest dilevery delays?
(2) Which warehouse has the highest number of delayed dileveries?
(3) What percentage of dileveries are completed on time versus delayed?
(4) Which warehouses have the highest average delay days?
(5) Are dilevery delays concentrated in specific warehouses or evenly distributed?
(6) What operational improvements can be reduce dilevery delays?

# Key Insights:
# Product Delay Distribution 
Fashion products recorded the highest number of delayed dileveries with 463 delayed orders, followed by sports 404 and Electronics 385.This suggests that certain product categories may face more logistical challenges.
# Dilevery performance:
Approximatey 55.65% of dileveries were completed on time, while 28.70% experienced delays of 1-2 days and 15.65% were delayed by more than 3 days.
# Warehouse Performance:
Among the three warehouses analyzed:
 Abuja warehouse recorded the highest average delay days (0.88), Lagos and Port Harcourt recorded lower average delay days (0.71).
 This suggests that operational factors at the abuja warehouse may be contributing to longer dilevery delays.
# Order Distribution by warehouse:
Port Harcourt recorded the highest number of On-Time dileveries with 392 orders, followed by Lagos 369 and abuja 352.
However, Abuja recorded the highest number of delayed dileveries in both the 1-2 days late and 3+ days late categories.
# Recommendations
Based on the analysis, the following actions are recommeded:
# Improve Abuja Warehouse Operations
Since Abuja recorded the highest average delay days and delayed orders, operational workflows should be review to identify inefficiencies in order processing and dispatch.
# Optimize Logistics for High-Delay Product Ctegories.
Fashion and sports products recorded the highest delay counts.Logistics teams should investigate possible causes such as packaging complexity, stock availability, or shipping routes.
# Strenghten Dilevery Monitoring Systems:
Implementing stronger dilevery tracking systems can help logistics teams detect delays earlier and take corrective actions.
# Standardize Best Practices Accross Warehouses:
Port Harcourt recorded the highest number on-time dileveries. Best practices from this warehouse could be analyzed and implemented accross other warehouses to improve overall perfomane.
